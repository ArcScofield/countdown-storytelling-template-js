    They're hauled on trucks and trains and stacked by the thousands onto freighters. They're the red corpuscles of international trade. They're called intermodal containers, and they're the basis of the unit by which container terminals are measured.

    The quintessential container is 20 feet long and 8 feet wide. Height varies, but most commonly measure 8 feet, 6 inches. Thus the term TEU: Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit. It's imprecise, since container dimensions vary. Many containers are double the length, but those are counted as one Forty-foot Equivalent Unit or two TEUs.

    When viewed from above, container ports are variations on a theme: Stockpiles of multi-colored containers; phalanxes of giant cranes; ships moored quayside or waiting in the wings.
